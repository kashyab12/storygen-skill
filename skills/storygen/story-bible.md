# Story Bible: The Living State System

A story is a living system of contexts. Just like software, a story breaks
when state isn't updated correctly — a character knowing something they
haven't learned yet, an object teleporting between scenes, a wound that
vanishes. The story bible is the single source of truth that prevents these
bugs. It is also the story's **revision history**: the process matters as
much as the output, so the bible preserves the iteration instead of
discarding it.

**Rules of use**

1. Create the bible in Phase 1 (Plan), before any prose.
2. Update it after every drafting or revision session — treat "bible updated"
   as part of the definition of done for a scene.
3. Every Braintrust reviewer receives the bible alongside the artifact under
   review (outline at Checkpoint A, draft at Checkpoint B).
4. When the bible and the draft disagree, that's a bug: fix one of them
   deliberately, never silently.

Keep the bible as a working file (e.g. `story-bible.md` next to the draft)
using this template. Delete sections that don't apply; never leave them
stale.

- One day…
- Because of that…
- Because of that…
- Until finally…
- And ever since then…

## Audience & format

  comic, ~40 panels / …]

## World seed
- Setting: [environment type] with [defining twist]
- Inhabitants: [who lives here]
- Runs on: [power/resource]

  [struggle/conflict]

  interact — its grounding in each canon, its costs, its limits]

## Characters

- Strengths:
- Weaknesses (incl. the flaw that fights the want):
- Core quality: [one-line essence]

- Arc: [starting state] → [ending state]
- Knows (updated per scene): [facts this character currently has]

|---|---|---|---|---|

## Scene ledger

## Shot/panel lists (visual formats)

## Open threads

## Continuity watchlist
